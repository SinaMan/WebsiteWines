The Traditional Face Of Crete

A Major Wine-making Area


Archanes (or Arhanes), a traditional settlement located in the heart of the major wine-making area of Heraklion, Crete. One of the most important wine-growing regions since Minoan times, through archaeological evidence which indicates the unique location & the climate of Archanes.

Archanes is an appellation on the island of Crete in the Aegean Sea. The small village of Arhanes, in the center of the appellation, is historically important to Greek viticulture: a 4000-year-old wine press from the Minoan civilization was unearthed recently in the village. Pale, aromatic red wines are made in Arhanes from the local Kotsifali and Mandilaria grape varieties. Arhanes can be found between the Peza and Dafnes appellations, 8.5 miles (14km) southeast of Heraklion, Crete and 9 km south of the archaeological site of Knossos (Drive time: 25-35 minutes). Archanes won awards as the 2nd best restored village in Europe and the 1st in long term development prospects. Streets have been newly stone-paved, there are more splendid displays of blooming flowers, fruit trees and plants in the yards, terraces and balconies of the houses. There are four archaeological sites in the area - one inside the village (a fenced excavation) and three close by, at Anemospilia and Fourni (on the edge of the village - a Minoan graveyard). The area of ​​Archanes was known from Minoan period for producing high quality wine and olive oil. Vathypetro (Greek: Βαθύπετρο) is an archaeological site, 4 kilometres south of Archanes. The south sector of the building, which includes the wine press, was rebuilt as a farmhouse and industrial centre after the 1550-1450 bc and it is dated as one of the oldest in the Mediterranean. It is worthwhile to visit the area not only for the findings, but also for one of the most magnificent views of Crete.

Sinadinakis Winery

Sinadinakis winery is located in Archanes of Heraklion, Crete. The wines produced and bottled from the enologist Evangelos Sinadinakis and his family. In 1991, Sinadinakis decided to cultivate in the family vineyard several indigenous varieties of grapes such as Kotsifali (a local red variety which can be found only in the region of Archanes-Peza), Mantilari, Vilana combined with international varieties of Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Chardonnay, Sauvignon blanc and others, which gave us the raw material for vinifications in various variants. Hence, in 1997, the white Cretan wine Sinadinakis started, in 2000 the red Cretan wine Kotsifali and later on, in 2007 the red Cretan wine Merlot.

Vineyards & Location


The vineyards are located in the viticulture zone of Archanes-Peza, at four differnet locations 'Kolona', 'Loumata', 'Trapeza' and 'Vathypetro'. The crop is mild, with the use of organic methods and give 2 to 2.5 kg / vine ( 600-700 Kg / acre ). Small scale vinification with combination of traditional methods with those of modern enology. The climate of Archanes is Mediterranean with hot, dry summers and moderate winters. The hills that surround the village on the south and west side, protect the vineyards from the hot North African winds. Instead, in the north and east side the vineyards enjoy cooler breezes from the Aegean Sea (to the north), which temper the effects of hot sunshine and slow ripening, letting the grapes develop strong flavors and acidity in balanced measures. Archanes-Peza region is the only appellation in Greece that cultivates the Kotsifali grape variety used in our wines.

Recommended Trips

What to visit

Minoan Trails

Take the road to Youktas montain &
visit the Minoan Mansion in Vathypetro.
(Follow the trails on foot or by car)

View details

The temple of Afentis Christos in the top of the Youktas mountain offers a 360 degree panorama view! Fresh mountain air and the scent of Cretan herbs will fill your lungs along the way: oregano, thyme and sage. Recommend to follow the walking path to the mountain (Ask the local for directions)

Music & Art

Take the road to Peza (by car), to visit the Nikos Kazatzakis Museum (Myrtia) & the Musical Workshop Labyrinth (Houdetsi).

View details

Check opening hours since they can be closed. Recommend to visit the Houdetsi Music Festival (At the summer period). It is a 4-day celebration of music, dance, food and art that takes place in Houdetsi.

Food & Drink

Archanes are also famous for their restorants offering outdoor seating with traditional cretan specialities.

View details

In the square of Archanes you can find some exceptional restaurants to experience the Cretan specialities. "Kritamos" or "To Spitiko" are two places recommended by us. In the center of the square is the "Bakaliko" shop where you can drink a cup of tea or coffee with traditional cretan sweets and taste small mezedes, with a variety of local wines. Just at the edge of the square (in the old market of Archanes - on your right) two old Kafeneio "To Ploumi" and a little heighter "H Agora" are two good places to enjoy cretan traditional mezedes and sometimes hear some local music during the summer hot evenings..
